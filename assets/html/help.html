What makes Jigsaw3D challenging is moving around in 3D space.

To get started, we suggest you read this first, then try the Cube puzzle with the "Really Easy" level of difficulty.

Looking Around in 3D

Drag the background to view the puzzle from different angles and positions:

In rotate mode dragging will rotate the puzzle to let you see different sides.

In pan mode dragging will move the puzzle around.

You can zoom in and out with the zoom controls.

Tip: Double tapping the background will center the puzzle and zoom it so all the pieces are in view.

Tip: A long press will let you see what the puzzle looks like all put together.

Moving the pieces

Move the puzzle pieces by dragging them with your finger. When you drag a piece, its bounding box will "light up" with red and green lines.

What's a bounding box? A bounding box is a virtual box that surrounds each piece. It's the smallest rectangular box that will hold the piece.

When you drag a piece, the green lines surround the face you are touching. You can only drag the piece parallel to that face. For example, if you are touching the front face, you can only drag the piece up, down, left or right, not back and front. If you are touching the top face, you can only drag forward, backward, left and right, but not up and down.

Sometimes when a piece seems stuck, it's because the bounding box of another piece is in the way. You'll have to move that piece first.

When the piece is close to where it belongs in the puzzle, it will snap into place.

Hint Boxes

Those grey boxes in the center are the puzzle positions for the pieces, to help guide you. For more of a challenge, you can change the Hints to dots in the preference settings.

Good luck and enjoy!
